The Art of Matt Delcourt in the spaces that inspired it

Scroll to walk through abandoned places where nature has taken hold — and where Matt's paintings have found their true home.

Scroll to enter
I

The Factory Floor

Vines split the ceiling. Light pours through the breach. On the far wall, a deer skull rests beneath a glowing medallion — proof that the sacred outlasts the industrial.

II

The Gymnasium

The basketball hoop rusts. The teal paint peels in slow surrender. But here, above the rubble, a heart blazes between crossed revolvers — defiant, unapologetic, alive.

III

The Asylum

An armchair waits for someone who left decades ago. Through arched windows, the outside presses in. On the crumbling wall, two hands offer a heart to the dark — and the dark accepts.

IV

The Attic

A single chair. A single window. Warm light cuts through dust and silence. On the leaning wall, a figure sits in stillness, heart glowing through skin — meditation in a place the world forgot.

V

The Corner Room

Floral wallpaper peels to reveal green paint beneath. Through a broken corner window, the desert watches. Someone left these paintings here — or maybe the paintings chose to stay.

VI

The Hall

Three tall windows. Dusty curtains. An abandoned institution becomes a gallery — every heart painting catching the grey light, every frame a defiant act of beauty in a place that was supposed to be empty.

VII

The Last Room

The floor has given way. The ceiling sags. But here — on the wall, leaning against the baseboard, catching dust and light alike — the hearts remain. They were always going to outlast the building.

The heart outlasts the ruin.

Matt Delcourt
